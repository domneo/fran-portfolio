        title: O2. Research - Understanding User Roles & Permissions
        showSectionTitle: true
        blocks:
          - content: |
              To begin, we first engaged users of a higher level of command to obtain their chain of command.

              This was important as it allowed us to:

              * Match user group to module
              * Identify archetypes and device-access within each module
              * Eventually map out the default and master configuration so that the System can be used upon deployment

              From the research, we identified three primary archetypes within each module:

              * **Super Admin**: Needs overall visibility and ‘master controls’
              * **Manager**: Needs overall visibility and control within their domain
              * End User: Needs a straightforward and "noise-free" experience where only relevant tools are visible

              Depending on the module, the different archetypes have different devices to access the system - desktop or mobile. Additionally, we found out that the chain of command could be changed depending on the type of operations the users will undertake.

        title: Problem Statement & Reframing
        showSectionTitle: true
        blocks:
          - content: |
              #### A 'too-big' problem statement

              Patients use the online symptom checker to quickly identify and understand their current and/or underlying health conditions and be aware of how they can manage their situation.

              1. However, patients are unable to begin the search process using keywords of their choice or find suitable keywords to describe their symptoms. This process is worsened by preset search terms that use medical jargon that they may not understand.
              2. In the symptom checker questionnaire, patients are confused by the questions due to unclear content, and feel that the questions are not directed at trying to understand their symptoms.
              3. Additionally, patients encounter medical jargon they do not understand throughout the symptom checking experience - from the search input, to the questionnaire, and to the diagnosis.
              4. Patients perceive the website as less reliable and credible because they are unable to find information about website's contributors. They are also faced with an incomplete diagnosis upon completing the symptom checker questionnaire.

              Overall, these problems negatively affect Symplicured, with low conversion rates on the landing page, and high drop-off rates at various stages of the online symptom checking process.

              #### Reframing the Issue

              By allowing digital patients multiple ways to input their symptoms based on current observations and integrating visual aids into the website, we will empower them to quickly narrow down their condition to reach an accurate and personalised diagnosis. This will also help them be aware of what they can do to manage their symptoms.

              We know this to be true when there is an increase in the website's overall usability and user satisfaction, a higher conversion rate, and percentage of users completing the search process.

              ## Plan
            col2: |
              What type of information and how that information is presented to users makes a huge difference in boosting a site's credibility, and consequently, user conversion and retention

              * Usability and A/B tests with users showed that while transparency was important in establishing trust, we had to be aware of what and how information is presented
                * Example: On the landing page, testimonials and brand affiliations have to be placed intentionally or it will backfire
              * Design and layout changes should serve a clear purpose and help it stand out from a company's competitors
                * Example: On the diagnosis page, in 2 usability tests, users appreciated the upfront comparison of other potential diagnoses and thought it was "refreshing" that a symptom checker provided this easy-to-understand format
