          ## Background


          Storyblok has a component-based architecture that can help teams
          quickly put together web pages and publish them in a short time span.
          The company, however, was not utilising these benefits efficiently as
          content creation teams were heavily dependent on UX, Product, Tech
          teams to create various pages.


          This was an opportunity to not only onboard content creation teams to
          an untapped resource, but to also empower them to produce
          self-authored content by designing and building individual components
          that fully-utilise Storyblok’s capabilities. These components also
          needed to take on styles and guidelines from the Fortress Design
          System, while remaining visually cohesive when composed together in
          the CMS.

    title: 'O1. Collaborative Design: Awareness, Education, Workshops'
    showSectionTitle: true
    blocks:
      - content: >
          Together with a Product PM, we highlighted the opportunity that
          Storyblok could provide, and worked on educating the content creation
          teams on what a content management system is, how it can create pages,
          and its value to their work. This included freeing them from UX and
          Tech development sprints.


          We held workshops to identify what experiences they’d like to create
          and the relevant content types required to achieve those goals. They
          also identified from a competitor analysis the types of layouts and
          elements they looked for.


          From there, I designed the modular components and had design review
          sessions with the stakeholders to ensure that their needs are met. The
          Brand team was also there to ensure that the overall aesthetic was on
          brand. It was a reiterative process.

  - anchorId: launch_training_documentation
    title: 'O3. Launch, Training & Documentation'
    showSectionTitle: true
    blocks:
      - content: >
          We launched the Storyblok components starting with what was required
          for an MVP page, and staggered the release for the other components;
          20 components over 6 weeks. The staggered releases were intentional as
          it prevented the teams from becoming overwhelmed - teams had enough
          time to learn about and explore the components, and we were able to
          fix any issues we overlooked before the next batch was released.


          Each release was accompanied by a training session for content
          creations teams to become familiar with the components and learn to
          use them effectively. These were all documented in a living document
          that I co-write and co-maintain with the PM. The guide is regularly
          utilised by content creation teams for page creation, component use,
          and tracking. It provides:


          * An intro to Storyblok, Page fundamentals

          * Components available, how to use, and best practices

          * Reviewing and publishing guidelines

          * Tracking page success
