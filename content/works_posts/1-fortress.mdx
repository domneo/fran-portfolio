      - content: >
          Historically, the features and components on Castlery’s eCommerce site
          have been created and maintained on an on-demand basis without a UX
          team - the creative team designs visuals, and the frontend developers
          determine the requirements and hard-code these features. This led to
          inconsistent shopping experiences and UI, low web accessibility
          awareness, a “popup hell”, and long time to market for features and
          improvements. This was the situation I faced, as part of a newly hired
          2-person UX team.


          On one hand, we believed that shopping experiences should be
          consistent, intuitive, and on-brand regardless of our customers’
          device type. On the other, we wanted our developers to have a more
          efficient and effective way of developing and maintaining the
          eCommerce site across 3 markets. So, together with a senior frontend
          developer, we created the first design system for Castlery - the
          Fortress Design System, from the ground up.
