background: >
  ## Background


  Every year, Castlery runs many sales, events and campaigns in Singapore that
  are supported by various types of

  content pages (promotional pages, microsites, etc.). With the company’s
  expansion into Australia and the United States, the demand for these pages by
  Brand, Campaigns, SEO and Performance Marketing teams have only increased.


  A set of modular Content Management System (CMS) components was designed and
  developed to empower teams to self-author and publish different types of
  content pages independently, without support from the Product and Tech teams.
  This leverages our Content Management tool, Storyblok, delivering higher
  return on investment, while at the same time creating capacity for Product and
  Tech to work on other features.


  Before launch, the content pages had mixed visual styles, were not optimised
  for web and mobile, had no

  reference to a central design system, and had raw hex values in the code.
  Creating the content pages was also a time and labour intensive process that
  diverted resources from other projects. Due to the need for tech effort,

  promotional pages could only go live at the end of each sprint, imposing a
  strict timeline on the content teams; not all pages aligned with planned
  dates. The modular CMS components, along with documentation and training, were
  built to address these internal challenges.

          ## Limitations and constraints
        col2: >
          One huge requirement from the content creation teams was for the
          components to be highly customisable. The components will thus be
          subject to any (and every) kind of combination of copy types (e.g.
          header and/or subheader and/or description), rich text formatting, and
          colours. Ultimately, a design decision was made to adhere strictly to
          the brand’s assets, keeping the structural colours and font formatting
          as similar as possible to the main site.
