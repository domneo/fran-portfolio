          **Background, Problem**


          As a furniture company with an online-only presence in 2/3 markets, a
          majority of Castlery's customers are unable to view, touch, and
          compare furniture pieces easily. This meant that:


          1. Online customers are unable to easily visualize any 2 or more
          pieces styled together apart from predefined sets.

          2. In the showroom, customers are limited by our room styling and
          cannot easily rearrange large items.

          3. Our stylists, customer service and sales staff undergo
          time-consuming and tedious processes to aid customers who reach out
          for help.
        _template: oneColumn
      - content: |
          **Reframe**
        _template: oneColumn
      - col1: >
          How might we change the way customers shop and compare furniture
          online?
        col2: >
          Could we refocus customers from shopping for a product to styling a
          room?
        _template: twoColumn_1_1
      - content: >
          **Outcome**


          With a senior product manager, we envisioned a way to encourage
          customers looking at any product, to play with styling it in a room.
          This would help customers visualise how products would look in a room
          setting, helping them confirm their purchase decision, and supporting
          cross-sell product discovery leading to larger basket sizes.


          As such, we put together business cases to procure and integrate 3rd
          party products - Room Designer, Modular Configurator - into the
          website.
        _template: oneColumn
      - content: >
          **Roles & Responsibilities**


          1. UIUX designer responsible for the front-facing customer journey
          (upper to middle funnel)
             * Performed UX evaluations to support the business cases during the procurement process
             * Improve the end-to-end user experience and interactions of both 3rd party products
             * Identified lacking features and UX and UI heuristics
             * Prototyped improvements to the 3rd party products
             * Identify clear entry points into and out of the 3rd party products
             * Customers easily access the Room Designer and Configurator at various points of their shopping journey
          2. Product owner who maintains and manages the Fortress Design System
          implementation
             * Ensure that the 3rd party products are integrated with the Fortress Design System and is fully mobile-responsive
               * Customers have a cohesive experience regardless of device used
             * 3rd party products blend seamlessly into the website and adhere to our web design guidelines and branding
