    title: Background
    showSectionTitle: true
    blocks:
      - content: >
          Since conception, Castlery’s websites (across 3 markets) have always
          been designed and developed in an adaptive way - once for mobile and
          another for desktop. As such, users with device sizes that deviate
          from the preset sizes (e.g. tablets/wider mobile screens) would get
          subpar experiences on the e-commerce site. The adaptive design meant
          that tablet users were completely neglected.


          The company however, started to shift their focus to becoming
          mobile-first, with an increasing number of users accessing and
          purchasing from the site. There was also a growing interest in
          supporting tablet experiences as the sales teams 1. disliked the
          current POS system and 2. found it hard to show customers things in
          the showrooms on tablets (all sales usually done via tablet), and the
          company started to take a greater interest in creating
          online-to-offline (and vice versa) experiences. This was a huge chance
          to push for the shift from adaptive to responsive design.

    title: O1. Responsive web evangelism is sometimes about timing
    showSectionTitle: true
    blocks:
      - content: >
          When I had just joined Castlery, their adaptive design was an issue I
          had identified early, but it was hard to push the business agenda for
          adopting responsive web design.


          Only in 2023 did the company shift its focus to mobile-first as the
          numbers were showing increased mobile access and revenue. This gave me
          the opportunity to start advocating and having conversations about the
          value of mobile-first and device-accessibility, which aligned with
          other ongoing initiatives around offline-to-online and vice-versa
          (O2O) and omnichannel experiences.


          I also managed to get buy-in and commitment from Product and Tech
          teams to drive the initiative. I was paired with 2 front-end
          developers to overhaul the website, which was a lot of resource for a
          small company.
