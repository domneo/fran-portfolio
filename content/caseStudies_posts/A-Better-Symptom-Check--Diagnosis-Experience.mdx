  - anchorId: problem_reframe
    title: Problem Statement & Reframing
    showSectionTitle: true
    blocks:
      - content: >
          #### A 'too-big' problem statement


          Patients use the online symptom checker to quickly identify and
          understand their current and/or underlying health conditions and be
          aware of how they can manage their situation.


          1. However, patients are unable to begin the search process using
          keywords of their choice or find suitable keywords to describe their
          symptoms. This process is worsened by preset search terms that use
          medical jargon that they may not understand.

          2. In the symptom checker questionnaire, patients are confused by the
          questions due to unclear content, and feel that the questions are not
          directed at trying to understand their symptoms.

          3. Additionally, patients encounter medical jargon they do not
          understand throughout the symptom checking experience - from the
          search input, to the questionnaire, and to the diagnosis.

          4. Patients perceive the website as less reliable and credible because
          they are unable to find information about website’s contributors. They
          are also faced with an incomplete diagnosis upon completing the
          symptom checker questionnaire.


          Overall, these problems negatively affect Symplicured, with low
          conversion rates on the landing page, and high drop-off rates at
          various stages of the online symptom checking process.

          #### Reframing the Issue


          By allowing digital patients multiple ways to input their symptoms
          based on current observations and integrating visual aids into the
          website, we will empower them to quickly narrow down their condition
          to reach an accurate and personalised diagnosis. This will also help
          them be aware of what they can do to manage their symptoms.


          Furthermore, incorporating key information about the company and the
          site’s contributors, as well as providing more health-related
          information will help digital patients better determine the
          reliability and credibility of the website.


          We know this to be true when there is an increase in the website’s
          overall usability and user satisfaction, a higher conversion rate, and
          percentage of users completing the search process.

          ## Plan
        col2: >
          What type of information and how that information is presented to
          users makes a huge difference in boosting a site’s credibility, and
          consequently, user conversion and retention


          * Usability and A/B tests with users showed that while transparency
          was important in establishing trust, we had to be aware of what and
          how information is presented
            * Example: On the landing page, testimonials and brand affiliations have to be placed intentionally or it will backfire
          * Design and layout changes should serve a clear purpose and help it
          stand out from a company’s competitors
            * Example: On the diagnosis page, in 2 usability tests, users appreciated the upfront comparison of other potential diagnoses and thought it was “refreshing” that a symptom checker provided this easy-to-understand format
