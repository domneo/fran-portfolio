          ## Deep dive interviews
        col2: >
          Interviews with 4 MUJI shoppers revealed similarities – most notably,
          that they believed and wanted direct MUJI SG e-commerce. They were
          surprised that MUJI SG was an e-catalogue; the interviewees believed
          that e-catalogues served to direct consumers to luxury brands with
          in-store only experiences, not brands with daily use products. A huge
          pain point for interviewees was the difficulty they faced trying to
          navigate the MUJI SG site. They expected a brand’s in-store experience
          to carry over into its web counterpart, and was confused with the
          disconnect between MUJI’s online and offline experiences.

        col2: >

          In-store collection relieves delivery logistics and costs while
          providing consumers (who enjoy MUJI’s aesthetics) in-store experiences
          that cannot be attained online.\

        col2: >
          Offline and online experiences should ideally reflect each other and

          experiences should be like, iconic brands like MUJI can play to those
          strengths. This is especially since brand identities and experiences
          are self-affirming and mutually reinforcing.
